## Statistical power — how many reports until rankable

**Publication gate (k-anonymity).** A segment is withheld while it has `0 < reports < min_publish_n`. With `min_publish_n = 3`, a segment needs **3 reports** before it is published at all (segments with exactly 0 reports are published as a true zero).

**Distinguishing a segment from the median.** The published rates use the Byar/Poisson interval (`stats/rates.py`, docs/METHODOLOGY.md 5.2). Two segments are reported as *distinguishable* only when their 95% intervals do not overlap. The reference here is the median published segment, rate **5.00/1000** (95% CI 1.35–12.80, n=4). A candidate segment must lift its interval's lower bound above **12.80/1000** to clear it.

The count needed depends on the segment's exposure denominator (a busier segment needs more reports to reach the same rate). At the published exposure spread:

| Exposure level | Exposure | Reports to clear the median's CI |
| --- | ---: | ---: |
| lowest | 250 | 8 |
| median | 800 | 18 |
| highest | 1500 | 29 |
